# Unified Substrate Theory (UST)

An open-science research framework dedicated to the development, mathematical derivation, and evaluation of the Unified Substrate Theory (UST). This project serves as a centralized, public repository for formal manuscripts, foundational derivations, and supporting materials detailing the structural and dynamic properties of the substrate.

---

## Executive Summary

The Unified Substrate Theory (UST) introduces a comprehensive physical framework that models fundamental interactions, fields, and particles through the dynamics of a continuous underlying medium (the substrate). By utilizing advanced fluid-vortex mechanics and unique hybrid dispersion relations, the framework aims to reconcile current macroscopic cosmological observations with subatomic structures. 

### Core Research Areas:
* **Hybrid Dispersion Relations:** Modeling mathematical scaling laws across varying energy domains.
* **Fluid-Vortex Structures:** Defining the structural stability of particles as localized substrate excitations.
* **Cosmological Scaling Laws:** Analyzing core pressure distributions and halo mass calculations without relying on standard dark matter parameters.

---

## Repository Structure

To ensure complete transparency and ease of peer review, this archive is organized into dedicated functional directories:

* **`Books/`** – Comprehensive textbooks and extensive foundational literature detailing the theory.
* **`Derivations/`** – Step-by-step mathematical proofs, raw formulas, and verification sheets.
* **`Diagrams/`** – Structural illustrations, conceptual maps, and visual models of substrate dynamics.
* **`Papers/`** – Formal, academic-grade manuscripts prepared for community distribution and citation.
